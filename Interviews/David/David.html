(This transcript is a summary of each question and answer of the interview. It is not the exact interview)

Q. What is your current job, what projects are you working on and who do you work for?

A. Right now I work for a company called Athentek, A-T-H-E-N-T-E-K. I’m doing development for indoor tracking. We develop little tracking devices and infrastructure for high accuracy tracking, so if you were in a mall or hospital and wanted to know where precisely where you are, we are developing technology that would let you do that.

Q. How is that relevant to your degrees in aerospace and aeronautics?

A. My aerospace background was in gps, so I got my PhD in gps technology. That’s all location stuff. So when I got my degree and shortly thereafter it was for gps location, so between indoor and outdoor location. Tracking cars and planes and also animals.

Q. What are challenges you have run into?

A. The biggest one is that you don't even know what the problem is. When someone comes to you and say that they want a positioning system and you ask what the requirements are they say, “you tell me.” I have to come up with what the problem actually is, I have to come up with a way to see how well I’m doing and I have to come up with a way of testing it.

Q. Do you find that annoying, or do you enjoy the freedom it gives you?

A. That’s why I do this job, I’m not a person that wants to do the same thing day in and day out. The fact that I get to solve a unique problem that nobody else has solved is what keeps me coming back, and that I’m working on the whole thing, not a widget that is part of a larger system.

Q. What did you do for Lockheed Martin?

A. I did studies on space systems. It was special programs, which means it was classified work, but I was doing the same kind of computer analysis that engineers would do on an open project, I was just doing it on a closed project.

Q. What got you interested in all this stuff?

A. I think it was probably two things; I liked cars as a kid. I got my first car when I was 16 and worked summer jobs and got my second car when I was 18. I did a lot of work on them. I replaced parts and went drag racing. My dad, before he would give me driving lessons gave me a maintenance book and said, “first read these chapters,” so not college degree or anything I had to read this big automotive textbook thing. And the second thing was my part-time job when I was in my senior year of high school was at an engineering firm. And the guy was building boats and sonar systems to study the reservoirs of dams. And I thought it was really cool.

Q. If you were offered the opportunity to go to space would you say yes?

A. Oh yes, definitely.

Q. And what would you look forward to doing while in space?

A. I think I would look down at the Earth. When I fly in an airplane I enjoy looking at the Earth, so I think I would spend a lot of time doing that.
