Pioneer 10

The Pioneer of Space Vehicles

On March 2, 1972 the Pioneer 10 was launched into space. It’s destination, Jupiter. During the last stage of its launch the Pioneer 10 reached a speed of 52,000 kilometers per hour. It was the fastest man-made spacecraft to leave Earth. The Pioneer 10 was going so fast that it could go by the moon only 11 hours after launch and went passed Mars in only 12 weeks.
Four and a half months later the Pioneer 10 arrived at the asteroid belt. Soon afterwards it reached Jupiter, capturing the first close-up pictures of this massive planet. The Pioneer 10 discovered much about Jupiter that was previously unknown during this section of its long-trip. Once Pioneer 10 passed Jupiter it continued on to the outer reaches of the solar system and kept on giving us valuable information about the Sun and our solar system. On March, 31 1997 Pioneer 10 concluded its mission and on February 7, 2003 we finally lost contact with Pioneer 10.

~~~

The Pioneer 10 was one of the first space vehicles of all time, and was sent into space a mere 3 years after Apollo 11. It helped us learn more about planets like Jupiter and took beautiful pictures of them. It was the beginning of revolutionary space vehicles, it was the “Pioneer” of space.

Voyager

The Farthest Probe Out In Space

The Voyager is a spacecraft with a three-axis stabilizing system. This spacecraft is able to maintain high-gain antennas pointing at earth. A Flight Data Subsystem (FDS) is used to record data and can control the instrument’s operation.
The Voyager has traveled into interstellar space after spending over eleven years studying around Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. There are currently two voyagers: voyager 1 and voyager 2. Voyager 1 became the most distant human-made object in space in 1998 and exited the solar system in 2012. Voyager also has a number of symbols that are meant to represent human lifestyle and culture if it ever comes into contact with alien life.

~~~

The Voyager had important effects on how the space industry functions today. Although, each Voyager was only to last five year, the Voyager’s life spans grew to twelve years because of their great capabilities.

SpaceShipOne

First Manned Vehicle to have Suborbital Flights

In 2004 SpaceShipOne completed its first manned spaceflight. SpaceShipOne was designed by Burt Rutan (head of Scaled Composites), who claims that the ideas for this project began as early as the 1990's. SpaceShipOne is a research rocket and was made for suborbital flights. It was an anomaly of its time, having a "care-free" configuration allowing an easy re-entry. Soon after its first flight SpaceShipOne received a $10 million Ansari X Prize and immediately retired from active service.
The White Knight is the mother-ship to SpaceShipOne, it is a manned research aircraft designed for high-altitude missions. It is a carrier aircraft and was also designed by Scaled Composites. The White Knight's first flight was on August 1, 2002.

~~~

When these two space vehicles were created, nearly thirty years after the Pioneer 10, they were an anomaly. They were not only manned, but could fly, in space, around the world! It was such an achievement that it retired from service immediately after its first flight.

Curiosity Rover

The Rover that Mapped Mars

One of the most well-known, unmanned space vehicles ever was the "Curiosity" rover. This rover was launched from earth for a one-way trip to Mars on November 26, 2011. On August 6, 2012 it landed on the red-planet, its destination. This entire expedition was for NASA's Mars Exploration Program.
The Curiosity Rover's main job was to figure out if Mars ever did or ever will be able to be inhabited. It told us all about Mars's current and past environment. This rover analyzed tons of the rocks and minerals on Mars's surface to discover the planet's geology and climate. It even discovered the Red Planet contains about 2 percent water by weight. That means astronaut pioneers could extract roughly 2 pints (1 liter) of water out of every cubic foot (0.03 cubic meters) of Martian dirt they dig up.

~~~

We didn't know much about Mars before the Curiosity Rover. Without it, our knowledge of Mars would be a fraction of what it is now. It helped us understand what Mars was made of, it’s fundamentals. It also examined the planet's soil formation, structure, and chemical composition. Overall, it really helped us fully comprehend Mars.

Orion Spacecraft

Could Possibly be the first Manned Vehicle to Explore Deep Space

The Orion Spacecraft is designed to take humans to farther places than ever before. Orion is made for a team of astronauts to be able to explore deep space and sustain life aboard the Orion Spacecraft while doing deep space research. This spacecraft will provide emergency abort capabilities and is flexible enough to go on very long journies.
On December 5, 2014, Orion was launched for a space test. This test lasted four hours and tested the most critical safety components. In the future, NASA has said that Orion will launch on a new heavy-lift rocket that would be more powerful than any other rocket ever built. Exploration Mission-1 is set to be the first mission to have Orion integrated with this new space launch system.

~~~

This spacecraft could be a huge milestone for space exploration. The farthest a human has ever gone in space was back in April 1970, when the crew of NASA's Apollo 13 mission swung around the far side of the moon, putting them 248,655 miles away from Earth. With the Orion Spacecraft, we could possibly shatter this record.

New Shepard

The Recyclable Space Vehicle

The New Shepard Spacecraft is a capsule on top of a booster. The capsule can hold 6 people and has the largest windows in space. Meant to be reusable over and over again, the capsule which can seat six astronauts deploys parachutes as it falls to Earth, allowing a safe and secure landing for the capsule. In addition the booster rocket has landing gear which it deploys as it comes back to Earth so that it can be reused again. New Shepard’s main focus is to cut the cost of spaceflight which it does by allowing itself to be used again and again.
The New Shepard can exert 110,000 lbs of thrust and uses the B3 engine. The New Shepard was developed and is under the ownership of Amazon.com. The founder Jeff Bezos wanted the New Shepard for suborbital space tourism. The building and the development of this space vehicle went so well that the first commercial passengers flight are scheduled for 2018.

~~~

A recyclable and reusable rocket is very important for the environment. The amount of resources it takes to construct a space vehicle over and over would have great opposition from groups such as the EPA. Not to mention the cost, rocket and part of the vehicle can cost many millions of dollars. The design of the New Shepard to be able to be reused will transform many minds about the future of the space industry.

Ascender

Could be the Spacecraft to Successfully take off Horizontally

The Ascender, a spacecraft designed similarly to other planes, it is designed to take off and land horizontally. Like traditional aircraft it is supposed to use airports to take off and land. The ascender first will use 2 jet engines to ascend to a height of 26,000 feet before switching to rocket power in order to reach a distance of 62 miles above Earth’s surface before returning with its jet engines. Ascender was designed to be the first sub-orbital plane to carry passengers to space.

~~~

The most dangerous part of a spacecraft’s journey is at the beginning, during the take off. We are defying gravity and going straight upwards, it is incredibly dangerous and the most deaths have happened during the take-off stage. The Ascender could be revolutionary, the first spacecraft to horizontally take off. This could save lives and bring the number of mission failures down considerably. If the ascender is successful it will revolutionize the space industry.

Delta II

An American Expendable Launch System

The Delta II space vehicle entered service in 1989. The Delta II was an expendable launch vehicle that carried various items into space. In 1988, the Air Force ordered 20 of these vehicles and NASA purchased Delta IIs for the launch of three Earth-observing satellites. The Delta II has had 98 consecutive successful launches and there are two more launches expected in the next two years. Delta II has been given the name of “NASA’s workhorse” for its capability to carry payloads to various locations in space.
This unmanned rocket has carried and delivered a significant quantity of various items including the Mars Exploration Rovers. Over the many years the Delta II has increased in size and payload capability. The Delta II at first, could boost 100 pounds into earth’s low orbit; currently the Delta II has the capability to boost approximately 2,490 pounds.

~~~

The Delta II, throughout its existence has innovated and through technological advancements, has been NASA’s first choice in payload carriers. Throughout the Delta II’s almost 30 years of service, space exploration has expanded from the abilities of the Delta II.
