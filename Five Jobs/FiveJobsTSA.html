Payload Specialist

What Do They Do?

Payload specialists are added to a crew of astronauts for a specific payload, or task. They have one important job that they must do. They are technical experts selected to perform a single specific mission. They do not have to be US Citizens or even trained by NASA.

Requirements

  • You must undergo rigorous training in your specific task.

Is It For You?

Payload specialists have one major job, and they must be very good at that one job. This job is not for people who are jacks of all trades, masters of none. You must be trained in your task, and the outcome of that task is completely your responsibility.

Pilot Astronaut

What Do They Do?

Pilot astronauts are commanders, in the space shuttle and in a space station. They are responsible for a lot of things, like the outcome of a mission, the lives of their crew, and the safety of the flight. They help control and operate the space shuttle and can assist in other jobs.

Requirements

  • Bachelor’s Degree in engineering, biological science, physical science, or mathematics.
  • At least 1,000 hours pilot-in-command time in jet aircraft.
  • Ability to pass a NASA space physical:
    • Eyesight: 20/100 uncorrected. Should be correctable to 20/20.
    • Blood Pressure: 140/90 BPM in a sitting position.
    • Height between 62 and 75 inches.

Is It For You?

Are you a natural born leader? Do you like to be in charge? Do you feel you can be responsible enough to have others’ depend on you? This job requires you to make smart decisions and take responsibility for your actions. You must be a good leader if you want this job.

Aerospace Engineer

What Do They Do?

Aerospace engineers design aircrafts and spacecrafts with their knowledge of mechanics and electronics. You could design missiles, probes, and even space shuttles. Aerospace engineers are also responsible for testing their creations.

Requirements

  • Bachelor’s Degree in an aerospace engineering program.
  • Basic knowledge in electronics, principles of flight, physics, thermodynamics, propulsion, and materials science.

Is It For You?

Aerospace engineering requires mechanical, mathematical, and scientific abilities. If you do not like one of these topics, you should not venture into this field. But if you do, and you enjoy making and testing vehicles, you should maybe give this field a try!

Mission Specialist

What Do They Do?

Mission specialist astronauts have a lot of responsibility in a spacecraft. They take responsibility for systems, cre activity planning, usage of consumables, and operations. They perform other activities too, like spacewalks and are responsible for these types of operations. They are trained the details of all of their space shuttle's systems and their mission requirements and objectives.

Requirements

  • Bachelor’s Degree in engineering, biological science, physical science, or mathematics. Degree should be followed by at least 3 years of responsible experience.
  • Ability to pass a NASA space physical:
    • Eyesight: 20/100 uncorrected. Should be correctable to 20/20.
    • Blood Pressure: 140/90 BPM in a sitting position.
    • Height between 58.5 and 76 inches.

Is It For You?

Much like Pilot Astronauts, Mission Specialists have a lot of responsibility. You must be a responsible person if you want this job. You must be able to remember a lot of what is on the space shuttle and how to use its systems. It is a difficult job, and you must know a lot. Your crew’s lives probably will depend on you.

Aeronautic Maintenance

What Do They Do?

Aeronautic maintenance workers are quite important. They maintain the air or spacecraft, support equipment, and keep track of forms and records. They use their technical knowledge to diagnose and solve problems. They also keep track of their vehicle’s inventory and do many other jobs.

Requirements

  • High School courses in pneudraulics, physics, and electronics are desirable.
  • Knowledge of aircraft systems, maintenance, technical data, proper handling and use of hazardous waste and materials.

Is It For You?

This field requires a vast amount of knowledge in tools and technical data. You must be able to identify issues and be able to solve them, your crew’s lives could depend on you.
